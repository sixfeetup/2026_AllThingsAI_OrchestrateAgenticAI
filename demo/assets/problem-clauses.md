# Problem Clauses — Answer Key

Seven deliberately planted issues in the generated contract, plus one easter egg.

---

## 1. Contradiction: IP Ownership

- **Section 4.1** says Client owns *exclusively* all Deliverables and work product. "This assignment is exclusive and irrevocable."
- **Section 12.12** says Consultant *retains* IP rights and grants Client only a "non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license."
- These two clauses directly contradict each other on who owns the work.

## 2. Impossible Date

- **Section 2.5** requires delivery of the Phase 1 final report by **February 30, 2026**.
- February 30 does not exist in any year.

## 3. Incorrect Name

- **Section 12.13** refers to the Consultant as **"Six Feet Down, Inc."** instead of "Six Feet Up, Inc."
- This could void the clause or create ambiguity about which entity is bound.

## 4. Ambiguous Timeframe

- **Section 2.3** says work "shall commence promptly" and proceed with "all deliberate speed."
- No specific start date, milestone, or definition of "promptly" is provided.
- Compare with Section 2.5 which at least attempts a specific date (albeit an impossible one).

## 5. Ambiguous Terms — "Reasonable Effort"

- **Section 2.7** defines Consultant's obligation as "reasonable effort" three separate times.
- No definition of what constitutes "reasonable effort" — no metrics, no SLA, no benchmark.
- This makes the performance obligation essentially unenforceable.

## 6. Ambiguous Licensing

- **Section 4.3** grants Client a license to use Consultant's pre-existing tools/frameworks embedded in Deliverables, but says the "scope, exclusivity, duration, and transferability of this license shall be as mutually understood by the Parties."
- "Mutually understood" is not a defined term and no written agreement is required.
- Combined with the 4.1 vs 12.12 contradiction, IP ownership is deeply unclear.

## 7. Ambiguous Staffing

- **Section 13.1** requires "adequate staffing levels" with Personnel who possess "necessary skills and experience."
- No headcount, no named roles, no minimum seniority, no team composition.
- "Adequate" and "necessary" are subjective and undefined.

---

## Easter Egg: The Cupcake Clause

- **Section 12.10** (Force Majeure) buries an obligation for Consultant to deliver **500 cupcakes per month** to Client's office.
- The cupcake obligation explicitly *survives* force majeure events.
- Must include "at least three (3) seasonal varieties per quarter."
- Hidden in the middle of otherwise standard force majeure boilerplate.
