# Document Review Summary

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Document:** Master Services Agreement — L&LL LLC / Six Feet Up, Inc.

---

## Overview

This appears to be a standard Master Services Agreement between L&LL LLC (as Client) and Six Feet Up, Inc. (as Consultant) covering professional technology services. The agreement follows a fairly typical structure for engagements of this type and covers the expected areas including scope of work, payment terms, intellectual property, confidentiality, and liability.

## Key Observations

**Intellectual Property:** The IP assignment language seems somewhat broad. The agreement addresses ownership of work product, but the scope of what constitutes assignable IP may want to be tightened up depending on the nature of the engagement. It might be worth having counsel review the IP provisions to ensure they align with the parties' commercial intent.

**Indemnification:** The indemnification provisions appear to lean more heavily on one party than the other. This is not uncommon in agreements of this nature, but the Client may want to consider whether the balance of risk allocation is appropriate for the size and scope of the engagement.

**Staffing and Personnel:** The language around staffing commitments seems somewhat vague. It may be helpful to include more specific terms around personnel qualifications, key-person provisions, or approval rights for assigned team members. Consider clarifying expectations here.

**Limitation of Liability:** The liability cap and exclusion of consequential damages appear generally standard for a services agreement. These provisions seem reasonable, though the Client should confirm the cap amount works given the anticipated contract value.

**Payment Terms:** The payment structure and invoicing process look fairly standard. No major concerns noted, though the parties may want to confirm alignment on timing expectations.

**Term and Termination:** The termination provisions appear to provide both parties with reasonable exit options. The notice periods and wind-down terms seem within normal range for this type of agreement.

## Conclusion

Overall, the agreement appears to be a reasonably well-drafted MSA that covers the major areas you would expect. The provisions reviewed are largely consistent with market-standard terms for technology services engagements. The main areas that may warrant further attention are the breadth of the IP assignment language, the balance of the indemnification obligations, and the specificity of the staffing commitments. We would recommend having outside counsel do a final review before execution, but no critical red flags were identified.
