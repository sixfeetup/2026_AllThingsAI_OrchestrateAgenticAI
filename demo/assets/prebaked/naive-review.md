# Document Review Summary

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Document:** RFP 20-020 — Personal Property Tax Online Filing System (Indiana DLGF)

---

## Overview

This is a Request for Proposal issued by the Indiana Department of Administration on behalf of the Department of Local Government Finance for a personal property tax online filing system. The RFP package appears to be fairly standard for a state government procurement of this nature and size. It follows a generally well-structured format with sections covering general information, proposal requirements, and evaluation criteria, along with several supporting attachments addressing cost proposals, reference forms, and contractual terms.

## Key Observations

**Scope of Work:** The scope of work sections appear to cover the expected areas for a project of this type, including system development, implementation, hosting, and ongoing support. The requirements seem generally comprehensive, though some of the functional specifications could potentially benefit from additional specificity. It may be worth reviewing whether the scope descriptions provide sufficient clarity for respondents to develop accurate proposals.

**Evaluation Criteria:** The RFP includes an evaluation methodology with weighted scoring categories, which is typical for state procurements. The criteria appear to address technical capability, cost, and business qualifications. The weighting seems reasonable overall, though respondents may want to carefully review the scoring methodology to ensure their proposals are appropriately tailored to the areas of highest weight.

**Cost Proposal Structure:** The cost proposal template appears to be structured across multiple categories including implementation, hardware/software, hosting, and ongoing support. This multi-tab approach is fairly common for IT procurements. Respondents should review the template carefully to ensure all cost elements are captured in the appropriate sections.

**Contract Terms:** The RFP includes a contract template attachment that addresses the standard areas you would expect — term, termination, data security, and service levels. The provisions appear to be generally in line with typical state government contracting requirements. It might be worth having legal counsel review the specific terms before submission to ensure alignment with your organization's risk tolerance.

**Compliance and Certification Requirements:** The RFP includes various compliance requirements around minority and women-owned business enterprises, veteran-owned small business commitments, and other standard state certifications. These provisions appear consistent with Indiana procurement regulations and should not present unusual challenges for most respondents.

**Timeline and Milestones:** The RFP outlines a procurement timeline and references target implementation dates. The dates appear to follow a reasonable progression for a project of this scope. Respondents may want to consider whether the suggested timeline aligns with their delivery capabilities and factor in appropriate contingency time.

## Conclusion

Overall, RFP 20-020 appears to be a well-organized government procurement document that covers the major areas you would expect for an IT system development and implementation project. The requirements, evaluation process, and contractual framework are largely consistent with standard state procurement practices. The main areas that may warrant closer attention are ensuring full alignment between the cost proposal template and the scope of work, and confirming that the proposed timeline is achievable. We would recommend having outside counsel review the contract template attachment prior to proposal submission, but no critical red flags were identified.
