# All Things AI 2026: Strategy for a 45-Minute Session

Based on the 2026 conference structure, tracks, and audience profiles, this
document outlines the optimal approach for the "Agentic Quest" presentation.

## 1. Audience Demographics & Track Selection
The conference is divided into seven distinct tracks. Your session bridges
several, but the strongest fits are:

*   **Primary Track: AI Governance, Security & Compliance**
    *   *Why:* The "Ranger" role and the "No-Miss Review" focus directly on risk
      mitigation.
    *   *Audience:* Compliance officers (Clerics), CISOs, and senior Ops
      leaders.
*   **Secondary Track: Case Study / Demo**
    *   *Why:* The live "Dungeon Map" walkthrough is a high-signal demo.
    *   *Audience:* Mixed technical and business stakeholders.
*   **Alternate Track: AI Builders / Engineers**
    *   *Why:* The "Wizard" role and the technical orchestration patterns (MCP,
      State Machines).
    *   *Audience:* Developers and ML practitioners.

**Strategic Recommendation:** Position the talk for the **Governance &
Security** track. It is the most underserved area for "Agentic" content and
provides the highest value for your "Trust" messaging.

## 2. 45-Minute Timing Breakdown (Optimized for Engagement)
The "Conference Day" sessions are typically 30 minutes, but a 45-minute slot
(often reserved for "Deep Thought" or specific deep-dives) requires a different
pacing:

*   **00:00 - 05:00: The Tavern (Hook)**
    *   Open with the "Broken Promise." Show a slide of a "hallucinated"
      contract clause that would have cost $1M.
    *   Introduce the RPG Party metaphor as a *governance framework*, not just
      flavor.
*   **05:00 - 15:00: The Quest Board (Theory & Frameworks)**
    *   Introduce OODA and Cynefin.
    *   Define the "Graduated Trust" levels (1, 2, and 3). Explain that
      "Autonomous" (Level 3) is a destination earned through "Verification"
      (Level 2).
*   **15:00 - 35:00: The Dungeon (Live Demo)**
    *   *Phase 1:* The "Level 1" failure. Run a basic prompt; show it missing
      Trap #1.
    *   *Phase 2:* The "Level 2" Success. Run the Orchestration Task Graph. Show
      the "Adversary" agent catching the trap and the "Validator" ensuring
      coverage.
    *   *Phase 3:* The Engineering Parallel. Briefly show the Code Review task
      graph to satisfy the "Wizards."
*   **35:00 - 40:00: The Loot Table (Actionable Takeaways)**
    *   Recap the `CLAUDE.md` standard and the containment strategy.
    *   Provide the GitHub link to the "Playbook" repository.
*   **40:00 - 45:00: Q&A (The Campfire)**

## 3. High-Signal "Vibe" for All Things AI
*   **Open Source Roots:** All Things AI (formerly All Things Open) values
  "Open" tech. Emphasize that your orchestration patterns (MCP, JSON schemas)
  are **provider-agnostic** and avoid vendor lock-in.
*   **Hands-On & Practical:** The audience expects to see code/terminal. Avoid a
  100% slide-based presentation. The "Warforged" terminal demo is essential.
*   **The Durham Factor:** The audience is a mix of Research Triangle Park (RTP)
  enterprise folks and startup builders. The talk must balance **Enterprise
  Compliance** with **Startup Speed.**

## 4. Risks to Mitigate
*   **Network Failure:** All Things AI is held in a historic theatre/convention
  center. WiFi is notoriously spotty. **Recommendation:** Use pre-recorded
  terminal sessions (asciinema) as a hot-swap fallback.
*   **Demo Jargon:** Don't get bogged down in legal definitions during the
  contract demo. Focus on the **structural failure** (e.g., "The system missed a
  180-day notice period") rather than the legal theory.
*   **Metaphor Fatigue:** If the audience is mostly "Executives," pull back on
  the RPG names (Cleric/Ranger) and use their professional equivalents
  (Compliance/Security) as the primary titles, with the RPG names as
  "subtitles."
